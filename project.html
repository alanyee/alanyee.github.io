Basically, sustainability measures what pieces are still left by the endgame. The king always survive since that piece cannot be eaten, while none of the most common endgames ended with a second copy of a knight, bishop, and rook. If you are winning, at least one pawn is basically guaranteed to have at least one pawn in your arsenal. Though since this data spans from 1475 to 2014, this graph may not be entirely pertinent for the modern metagame, but still holds value for the history of chess and the game itself.
