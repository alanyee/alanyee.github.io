The Sustainability of Pieces

This graph shows a piece's sustainability â€” the likeliness a piece will survive until the endgame. The king always survive since that piece cannot be eaten, while none of the common endgames ended with a second copy of a knight, bishop, and rook. If you are winning, pawn are basically guaranteed to have at least one pawn in your arsenal. If you have a rook, your enemy is likely to have a rook; this applies vice versa, and such a mirror scenario occurs for the players' possession of their respective queens. Winning side has a little over 11% in sustainability for their bishops than the losing side's, while the losing side's is 8% more likely to live. This information speaks to the common theory that bishops work better than knights, on their own, as knights synergize with the movements and positions of the pawns, and as the game progresses, both sides start losing their pawns.
